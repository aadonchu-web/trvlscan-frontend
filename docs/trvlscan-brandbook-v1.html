TRVLscan
Brandbook.

The strategic and visual foundation of TRVLscan — a global flight aggregator where you pay in crypto and fly anywhere. Skyscanner speed, Wise trust.

01 · Strategy

If you have crypto,
you have the world.

TRVLscan exists for one reason: to remove the friction between holding crypto and travelling the world. No banks, no FX games, no card forms, no waiting. Search. Pay. Fly.

Mission
Make global travel buyable with stablecoins.

Today there are billions of dollars sitting in stablecoin wallets that can't directly buy a flight. We bridge that gap — connecting crypto holders to 500+ airlines worldwide through a single search.

Promise
Search. Pay. Fly.

Three steps. Less than five minutes. From entering your route to receiving your e-ticket. We compete on speed and simplicity, not on luxury.

01 · Audience
Mass-market crypto holders

Anyone with USDT, USDC, or other stablecoins. Not just web3 natives — also remote workers paid in crypto, businesses in restricted markets, holders looking for utility.

02 · Position
Skyscanner × Wise

The breadth and search UX of a flight aggregator. The trust, transparency, and warm clarity of a fintech app. Direct, helpful, never showy.

03 · Moat
Crypto-native checkout

We don't bolt crypto onto a travel site. The whole funnel is built around it — pricing, payment, confirmation, ticket delivery. That's why it's fast.

04 · Roadmap
Choose your stablecoin

Today: USDT on Tron. Tomorrow: USDC, BTC, ETH, BNB. The user picks the pair, we handle the conversion at live rates. The brand is built crypto-agnostic from day one.

02 · Voice & Tone

Direct. Clear. Helpful.

We sound like a smart friend who happens to know the travel and crypto industries — never like a hedge fund pitch deck or a luxury concierge brochure. If a sentence makes us sound clever, we cut it.

Yes — write like this
"Pay in crypto. Fly anywhere."

Concrete. Five words. Action + outcome. The reader knows exactly what they get.

No — never write this
"Cryptographic precision meets global mobility."

Vague. Performative. Sounds clever but says nothing about what you can do.

Yes
"Send only USDT on Tron. Other tokens will be lost."

A direct safety warning that protects the user's money. Specific token, specific network, specific consequence.

No
"Refined trust through invisible service layers."

Marketing-speak. We never use words like "refined", "curated", "elite", "concierge", "ecosystem".

Yes
"Choose your stablecoin. We'll handle the conversion."

Future-friendly framing. Works today (USDT only) and works tomorrow (USDC, BTC, ETH). Never write copy that pins us to a single token.

No
"USDT TRC-20 — the only way to pay."

Locks the brand into one rail. When we add USDC or BTC, every page with this copy needs rewriting. Avoid the trap.

The "crypto" vs "USDT on Tron" rule

Marketing copy uses broad terms. UI in payment moments uses precise ones. This protects future flexibility (USDC, BTC, ETH) without ever risking that someone sends the wrong token.

03 · Logo

One logo. Two colours.
Built for speed.

The TRVLscan logo combines a location pin (with motion lines) and a two-tone wordmark. The pin signals "where" — the motion lines signal "fast scan". Navy + bright blue keeps it readable on any travel context.

Clear space
Always 1× the pin height

Reserve clear space around the logo equal to the height of the pin mark. Nothing — text, image, edge — should enter this zone.

Minimum size
Don't go below 24px tall

For the full lockup, never render below 24px in height (digital) or 12mm (print). Below that, use the pin mark only.

Backgrounds
White, surface, navy only

On dark backgrounds, the "TRVL" portion shifts to white. The "scan" portion stays bright blue. The pin keeps its gradient.

Don't

×
Don't recolour the wordmark — TRVL stays navy, scan stays blue.
×
Don't stretch, skew, rotate or add drop-shadows.
×
Don't place over busy photos — only solid backgrounds.
×
Don't add taglines or sub-text inside the lockup.

07 · Imagery

Real travel.
Honest moments.

No staged luxury balconies, no AI-generated faces, no stock-photo handshakes. We show real people in real travel situations — the airport queue, the boarding pass on a phone, the rooftop in a foreign city. Or we use simple flat illustrations (like our destination cards).

Use

  • Flat illustrations of cities and landmarksLike the destination cards on the homepage. Limited palette, geometric, friendly.
  • Authentic travel photographyPhone in hand at a gate, a boarding pass, a rooftop view. Unposed, slightly imperfect.
  • Real screenshots of the productMarketing should show the actual UI, not faked dashboards.
  • Aerial & landscape shots without peopleSkylines, runways, mountains, oceans — anything that signals "going somewhere".

Avoid

  • Luxury hotel balconies at sunsetWrong audience. We're not a Six Senses concierge.
  • AI-generated portraits of "happy customers"Always look fake, erode trust, and the eyes never quite work.
  • Crypto clichésGlowing coins, bull/bear figurines, holographic chains, "blockchain" abstract networks.
  • Stock photo handshakes & suit-wearing meetingsWe are not a financial services firm pitching to a board.
08 · Components

Generous radius.
Soft shadows. Honest borders.

Our components have rounded corners (12px default, 16px for cards, 24px for hero containers). Shadows are diffuse and tinted with navy, never plain black. Borders are 1px and used sparingly — usually we separate with surface luminosity instead.

Buttons

Primary = TRVL Blue, used once per screen for the main action. Success = USDT Green, only for confirmations. Secondary = surface fill, neutral. Ghost = no background, blue text.

Form inputs

Always show a label above the input. Placeholder shows an example, never an instruction.

Pills & badges
Verified USDT · TRC-20 Expires in 11:49 Round Trip

Pills are 999px radius. Always pair the colour with a clear meaning — green = verified/safe, blue = informational, amber = time-sensitive, neutral = filter or category.

Status messaging

Hero headline rule

The signature TRVLscan hero is built in two beats. Line 1 — "Fly anywhere." in Deep Navy. Line 2 — "Pay in crypto." in TRVL Blue with a USDT Green underline. Desire first, mechanism second. The green underline is reserved for this single phrase and never repeated elsewhere on the page.
