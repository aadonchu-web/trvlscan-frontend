§ 04 · Questions

The questions everyone sensibly asks first.

Don't see yours? Email support — a human replies within an hour during European working time.

Q.01 Is this a real airline ticket or a voucher?
A real ticket. The same e-ticket and PNR you'd get from the airline directly, issued through IATA-licensed settlement. You can manage it on the carrier's site — seat selection, baggage, the lot.
Q.02 Which networks and tokens do you accept?
On the payment screen the address is locked to one network at a time — today that's USDT on Tron (TRC-20). We're rolling out USDC on Ethereum and Solana next; sending the wrong asset to the wrong address will not be recoverable, so the UI won't let you guess.
Q.03 What's the fee, and where does the FX rate come from?
A flat 2.5% on top of the airline's published fare. The GBP→USD rate is the ECB-derived mid rate from frankfurter.app, refreshed hourly — not the punitive spread your card processor would apply at the till.
Q.04 What happens if my payment confirms after the timer expires?
We try to re-confirm the same fare for up to 30 minutes; if it's gone, we refund the USDT to the originating wallet and release the booking. No customer-service ticket required, no merchant credit, no "store balance."
Q.05 Do I need to create an account to book?
No. We need passenger details (the airline does, technically) and an email for the e-ticket. Nothing else — no password, no phone-OTP, no "verify your identity in 3 to 5 business days."
Q.06 Can I change or cancel after the booking is confirmed?
Whatever the airline allows, you can do — every ticket carries the carrier's own fare rules, shown to you before payment. Refunds (when the fare permits one) come back as USDT, calculated at the same FX rate you originally paid at, so you're not on the wrong side of a market move.
